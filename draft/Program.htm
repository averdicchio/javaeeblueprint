Big Smokes Cigar shop is located in Key West, Florida. Big Smokes has their own lines of cigars, but they also sell cigars from other manufacturers with whom they have established a relationship. Most of the customers for Big Smokes are walk-ins, calls, or mail orders from previous customers. The cigar industry is growing at a rate of 200 percent a year and Big Smokes wants to figure out how to join the growth. Big Smokes has established a goal to expand revenue by 100 percent in 12 months. To accomplish this goal and join in the industry growth, Big Smokes must expand their market reach throughout the world. Big Smokes has invested $2 million dollars to double the capacity of their warehouse. Now they are contracting with you to build them an Internet retail site to handle orders from all over the world.

Workshop Output
You are the architect for the retail site for Big Smokes. You have been tasked by Big Smokes management to lead the team responsible for the design, implementation, and ongoing management of the complete system as a turnkey or complete solution.
After an intensive series of discovery workshops with the in-house business analysts and subject matter experts, you know the following facts:
● Big Smokes has an existing inventory system that tracks all cigars they have in the warehouse.
● Big Smokes has an existing relationship with Merchant Bank to handle their credit card transactions. They use a point of sale (POS) system to manually enter a customer's credit card numbers and expiration date. Merchant Bank can handle Internet transactions.
● Big Smokes manually calls their manufacturers to check the availability of a cigar. This availability check should be automated. Each manufacturer has an inventory system that uses an industry standard interface based on the JavaTM Message Service (JMS).
● Big Smokes has existing relationships with Freight Movers and International Shipping for shipping cigars to mail-order customers. They want to continue using these relationships for shipping cigars on Internet orders. Freight Movers has a proprietary Java API for sending shipping requests. International Shippers uses a web service to accept shipping requests.
● System performance (Big Smokes wants to be an industry leader in site response, so they want 90 percent of all transactions to be under 5 seconds and no transaction exceeding 10 seconds), scalability (to 200 concurrent users), availability (99.99 percent during core working hours) and security (128-bit encryption at a minimum) are all key requirements and you must explicitly address each requirement in your proposed solution.

Use Case Specification – Search Catalog
Brief Description
The Search Catalog use case allows the customer to browse the catalog of products available.
Basic Flow
1. Customer selects a product category.
2. System responds with a list of products in the selected category.
3. Customer selects a product.
4. System responds with the product detail and availability of the product.

Use Case Specification – Add Product to Cart
Brief Description
The Add Product to Cart use case adds the specific product selected to the customer's shopping cart.
Basic Flow
1. Customer selects a product to add to the shopping cart.
2. System executes the Check Availability use case.
3. System adds the product to the shopping cart and displays the shopping cart to the customer.
4. Customer modifies the number of units to be purchased.
5. System calculates a new total amount and displays the updated page to the customer.

Use Case Specification – Check Availability
Brief Description
The Check Availability use case checks the inventory system to determine how many of a given product are available for sale.
Basic Flow
1. System sends a request containing the product ID to the inventory system.
2. Inventory system sends a response with the number of units available.
3. System sends the number of units available to the requesting use case.

Use Case Specification – Check Out
Brief Description
The Check Out use case allows the customer to purchase the items they have placed in their shopping cart.
Basic Flow
1. Customer selects check out.
2. System responds with a list of products in the shopping cart along with a subtotal.
3. Customer modifies quantity and selects a shipping method.
4. System updates the subtotal and provides a total amount with shipping costs.
5. Customer selects the credit card type and enters the number and expiration date.
6. System authorizes credit card payment, sends email confirmation and responds with a order number.
7. System sends shipping request to the selected shipping company.

Deliverables
It is your task to create an architecture and design for the System under Development (SuD) with the given business domain model, information provided above and requirements in the use cases. The architecture must be based on the Java, Platform, Enterprise Edition (Java EE) platform1. All deliverables will be accepted as HTML only and each diagram must be UML compliant.

1. Create a class diagram for the SuD. Public method names referenced in other UML diagrams (for example, sequence diagrams) should be provided.
2. Create a Component diagram for the SuD showing the components used in the system and their interaction. Examples of components are Enterprise JavaBeanTM (EJBTM), servlets, JavaServerTM Pages (JSPTM), major Plain Old Java Objects (POJOs), and important Managers / Controllers / Design Pattern implementations.
3. Create a Deployment diagram that describes the proposed physical layout of the major tiers of the SuD.
4. Create either a Sequence or Collaboration diagram for each use case provided.
5. List the top three technical risks you have identified in the project and identify a mitigation strategy for each risk.
6. List any assumptions made during the process of creating the architecture and design that have a material impact on your design and any decisions made in arriving at that design.

Your architecture and design will be graded on how well it supports the requirements detailed in this document and on the clarity of all information provided in both textual and diagrammatic form.
